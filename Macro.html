What is Macro Photography?

Macro photography is the practice of photographing small objects up close, such as bugs and flowers. Macro photography can be done in a studio or outdoors as long as the subject is adequately magnified. Officially, macro photography is defined as taking photographs of small subjects with a magnification of "life size" or larger.In a moment, I'll go into the definitions of magnification and life-size, but in essence, it means that your subject must be the same size as your camera sensor or smaller, and it must fill the frame. (For example, if your camera sensor is one inch wide, you'll be photographing objects that are one inch or smaller.) That is a rather stringent definition, and you will commonly hear photographers refer to a photograph as "macro" even if it depicts a somewhat larger subject. The same may be said for the photographs in this article, many of which do not meet this precise definition but are nonetheless close-up photographs.
