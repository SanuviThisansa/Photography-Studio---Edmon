What is Photography?

The term photography comes from the Greek words photo, which means light, and graph, which means to draw. Photography is the process of creating a photograph by capturing an image on light sensitive film or, in the case of digital photography, a digital electronic or magnetic memory.

More About Photography

Photographs can be found in almost every element of modern life. Photographs are used in newspapers, magazines, ads, posters, television, the Internet, passports, ID cards, archives, security and surveillance systems, forensics, and medicine as a means of communication and documentation. Photography is also used extensively in both home and leisure settings. The majority of images taken today are Snapshots commemorating events such as holidays and festivities. These actions are also documented for display on photo-sharing websites and photo-based Social Networking sites, thanks to the widespread use of digital cameras and mobile phone cameras. Despite the widespread use of photography in many parts of modern life, only a small percentage of photographs are deemed art and are presented in museums and galleries in similar forms to paintings. The origins of photography are a contentious topic. It was the result of a number of technological developments, most notably those associated with the Industrial Revolution in the nineteenth century, but it was also influenced by earlier technological developments such as the Camera obscura, an optical device used to aid drawing and perspective during the Renaissance.
